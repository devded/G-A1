# German Consonants


Important spelling-to-sound patterns:

- `w` sounds like English **v**: `Wasser`.
- `v` often sounds like **f** in native words: `Vater`.
- `z` sounds `/ts/`: `Zeit`.
- `j` sounds like English **y**: `ja`.
- final `b`, `d`, `g` are usually devoiced: `Abend`, `Tag`.
- `r` has several accepted German realisations; clarity matters more than forcing one regional version.

Do not insert extra vowels inside consonant clusters. Practise slowly, then connect the sounds.
