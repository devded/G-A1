# German Vowels


Vowel length can distinguish words and strongly affects intelligibility. Long vowels are often tense and held longer; short vowels are brief and more relaxed.

| Contrast | Example |
|---|---|
| long /iː/ vs short /ɪ/ | `ihm` / `im` |
| long /uː/ vs short /ʊ/ | `Schule` / `Mutter` |
| long /aː/ vs short /a/ | `Staat` / `Stadt` |

Double consonants usually signal that the preceding vowel is short. `h` after a vowel often marks length. Listen before relying on spelling rules.
