# Umlauts: Ä, Ö and Ü


- `ä` is often near the vowel in English *bed* or a long open `e`, depending on the word.
- For `ö`, shape the lips for `o` but place the tongue for `e`.
- For `ü`, shape the lips for `u` but place the tongue for `i`.

Bengali speakers should avoid replacing `ö` with ordinary `o` or `ü` with `u`. Use mirror practice: keep rounded lips while changing only tongue position.

Minimal practice: `schon–schön`, `musste–müsste`, `Bruder–Brüder`.
