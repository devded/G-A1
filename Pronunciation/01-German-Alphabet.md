# German Alphabet


German uses the 26 basic Latin letters plus `ä`, `ö`, `ü` and `ß`. Letter names matter for spelling names, addresses and email addresses.

| Letter | Approximate German name | Example |
|---|---|---|
| A | ah | Apfel |
| B | beh | Bus |
| C | tseh | Café |
| D | deh | Deutschland |
| E | eh | Essen |
| F | eff | Familie |

| X | iks | Taxi |
| Y | üpsilon | Yoga |
| Z | tsett | Zeit |

Practise spelling your full name, city and email address. Say `@` as `at` or `Klammeraffe`, and `.` as `Punkt` in guided beginner contexts.
