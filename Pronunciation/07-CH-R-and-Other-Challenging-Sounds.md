# CH, R and Other Challenging Sounds


German `ch` commonly has two A1-relevant sounds:

- `/ç/` after front vowels and consonants: `ich`, `Milch`.
- `/x/` after `a`, `o`, `u`, `au`: `Bach`, `Buch`, `auch`.

For `/ç/`, keep the tongue near the hard palate and let air pass without making `sh`. For `/x/`, use friction farther back, similar to a gentle throat sound.

German `r` may be uvular or vocalised. Do not add a strong Bengali-style trill if it distorts the word; imitate the model and prioritise vowel length and stress.
