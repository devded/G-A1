# Course Guide

## How to use the course

Follow the lessons in order. Later lessons assume that earlier pronouns, verbs, word order and cases have already been practised. Complete every skill section; do not treat listening or speaking as optional extras.

## How adults learn languages

Adults learn efficiently when new material is meaningful, connected to previous knowledge and retrieved repeatedly. Understanding an explanation is only the first stage. A useful progression is:

1. Understand the meaning and pattern.
2. Recognise it while reading or listening.
3. Produce it in a controlled exercise.
4. Use it in personal writing.
5. Use it while speaking without a full script.
6. Retrieve it again several days later.

Forgetting is expected. Each successful attempt to remember strengthens the memory.

## Vocabulary learning

Learn nouns as a package:

```text
der Tisch — die Tische — table — টেবিল
```

Learn verbs with a practical form and sentence. Learn frequent expressions as chunks: `Können Sie das bitte wiederholen?` is more useful than learning `wiederholen` alone.

### Active recall

Cover the answer and retrieve it before checking. Say the answer aloud. Recognition feels easy but production prepares you to speak and write.

### Spaced repetition

Use this default schedule:

| Review | Timing |
|---|---|
| 1 | later on the same day |
| 2 | one day later |
| 3 | three days later |
| 4 | seven days later |
| 5 | fourteen days later |
| 6 | thirty days later |
| 7 | Week 8 final review |

The CSV decks in `Flashcards/` can be imported into Anki. Paper cards are also fine.

## Grammar learning

For each topic:

1. Identify the communication purpose.
2. Study one clear structure.
3. compare several examples.
4. Complete a controlled exercise.
5. Make three personal sentences.
6. Use the pattern in a speaking task.
7. Review it later in mixed practice.

Avoid memorising large tables without sentences. Prefer simple correct German to advanced uncontrolled German.

## Shadowing

1. Listen once without text.
2. Read the transcript while listening.
3. Pause after short phrases and repeat.
4. Speak nearly together with the audio.
5. Record yourself.
6. Compare one feature: vowel length, stress, rhythm or final sound.
7. Repeat the whole line naturally.

Use five to ten focused minutes. All 56 daily lessons include a local audio file and transcript.

## Speaking alone

Use question-and-answer drills, role switching, picture description, routine narration and timed recordings. Keep a weekly recording so you can compare Week 1 with Week 8.

A strong one-minute routine:

- 15 seconds: introduce the topic.
- 30 seconds: give three or four details.
- 15 seconds: add a preference, time or reason.

## Listening

Use three passes:

- First pass: situation and general meaning.
- Second pass: names, numbers, time, price, place and action.
- Third pass: confirmation and one short dictation.

Do not open the transcript before making a genuine attempt.

## Reading

Predict from headings and layout, read once for the main point, then scan for the question's information. Look up only words that block understanding or are important A1 vocabulary.

## Writing

Plan required content first. Use short sentences, check the conjugated verb, capitalise nouns, check articles and read the final message aloud. Rewrite after correction; the second version is where much of the learning happens.

## English and Bengali support

English is the main explanation language. Bengali is used for meanings and for comparisons that genuinely help, especially grammatical gender, article changes, verb position and pronunciation challenges. Avoid permanent dependence on word-for-word translation.

## Dictionaries and translation tools

Use this order:

1. Guess from context.
2. Identify whether the word is a noun, verb or other type.
3. Check a dictionary.
4. Inspect an example sentence.
5. Use a translator only after your own attempt.
6. Record the exact difference you misunderstood.

## AI conversation practice

Use the prompt in `Speaking/AI_CONVERSATION_GUIDE.md`. The AI should use A1 German, ask one question at a time, wait for your answer and let you repeat a correction.

## Error log

Record repeated errors in this format:

| My sentence | Correct sentence | Type | Why | New example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ich kommen aus Dhaka. | Ich komme aus Dhaka. | verb ending | `ich` uses `-e` | Ich wohne in Ankara. |

At the end of each week, select the three most repeated errors and create new examples.

## Weekly review

Day 7 is active consolidation:

- closed-book vocabulary recall
- mixed grammar questions
- one familiar and one new listening task
- one speaking recording
- one reading passage
- one corrected writing task
- weekly quiz and error classification

## Avoiding burnout

Use the 80% rule: continue when the main concept is usable, and schedule weaker details for review. On a difficult day, complete the 30-minute minimum routine: ten minutes of cards, five minutes of listening, five minutes of grammar, five minutes of speaking and three written sentences.

Do not restart the course after missing days. Resume from the last completed lesson and use the weekly revision file to repair gaps.

## Common beginner mistakes

- Learning nouns without articles and plurals
- Translating German word for word from English or Bengali
- Moving the finite verb to the end of a normal German statement
- Forgetting verb endings
- Ignoring noun capitalisation
- Mixing `du` and formal `Sie`
- Avoiding speaking until “ready”
- Using many unrelated apps instead of one curriculum
- Adding new words without reviewing old ones

## What counts as progress

Measure can-do skills, not pages read. Mark a skill as:

- Not Started
- Learning
- Needs Review
- Can Do with Help
- Can Do Independently

You can mark “independently” only when you can perform without reading a complete model answer.

## Eight-week commitment

- Study at a consistent time when possible.
- Speak German every day.
- Attempt before checking.
- Correct every exercise.
- Review vocabulary on schedule.
- Record one speaking checkpoint weekly.
- Continue after mistakes and missed days.
