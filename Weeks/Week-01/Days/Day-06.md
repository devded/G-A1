## Step 2 — Grammar Bite (10 min)

<div class="grammar-box">

### sein vs haben in Everyday Expressions

### Comparing **sein** and **haben**

| Pronoun | sein (to be) | haben (to have) |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| ich <SpeakButton text="ich" /> | **bin** <SpeakButton text="bin" /> | **habe** <SpeakButton text="habe" /> |
| du <SpeakButton text="du" /> | **bist** <SpeakButton text="bist" /> | **hast** <SpeakButton text="hast" /> |
| er/sie/es <SpeakButton text="er/sie/es" /> | **ist** <SpeakButton text="ist" /> | **hat** <SpeakButton text="hat" /> |
| wir <SpeakButton text="wir" /> | **sind** <SpeakButton text="sind" /> | **haben** <SpeakButton text="haben" /> |
| ihr <SpeakButton text="ihr" /> | **seid** <SpeakButton text="seid" /> | **habt** <SpeakButton text="habt" /> |
| sie/Sie <SpeakButton text="sie/Sie" /> | **sind** <SpeakButton text="sind" /> | **haben** <SpeakButton text="haben" /> |

> <Icon name="target" /> **Expressing Feelings:**  
> • German uses **sein** for physical states: *Ich bin müde.* (I am tired).   <SpeakButton text="sein" />
> • German can also use **haben** + Noun: *Ich habe Hunger.* (I have hunger = I am hungry). <SpeakButton text="haben" />

</div>

## Step 3 — Practice (9 min)

### 3A — Complete the sentences

**1.** Ich ___ (haben) eine Frage. → <details><summary>Answer</summary>**habe** <SpeakButton text="Ich habe eine Frage." /></details>
**2.** Er ___ (sein) müde. → <details><summary>Answer</summary>**ist** <SpeakButton text="Er ist müde." /></details>
**3.** Hast du ___ (no) Geld? → <details><summary>Answer</summary>**kein** <SpeakButton text="Hast du kein Geld?" /></details>
**4.** Wir ___ (haben) Zeit. → <details><summary>Answer</summary>**haben** <SpeakButton text="Wir haben Zeit." /></details>
**5.** Bist du ___ (hungry)? → <details><summary>Answer</summary>**hungrig** <SpeakButton text="Bist du hungrig?" /></details>
