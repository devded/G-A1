# Week 7 Grammar — Health, Emergencies, Services and Recent Events

## Health


### Concept

German uses several fixed patterns for symptoms: Ich habe …, Mir ist … and … tut mir weh.

### Purpose

Describe a basic health problem and ask for medical help.

### Sentence structure

```text
Ich habe Kopfschmerzen. Mir ist schlecht. Mein Rücken tut mir weh.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich habe Fieber. | I have a fever. |
| Mein Bauch tut mir weh. | My stomach hurts. |
| Mir ist schwindelig. | I feel dizzy. |
| Ich brauche einen Arzt. | I need a doctor. |

### Common mistakes

- Translating every English “I am” symptom with ich bin.
- Forgetting mir in tut mir weh.
- Using advanced medical vocabulary instead of simple symptom descriptions.

### English/Bengali comparison

Bengali also uses several different constructions for symptoms; learn each German phrase as a chunk.

### Quick grammar check

1. Copy one example and change one detail.
2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.

## Integration


### Concept

Today combines previously learned grammar with a practical communication theme.

### Purpose

Move knowledge from controlled exercises into reading, listening, speaking and writing.

### Sentence structure

```text
Review known pattern → add theme vocabulary → practise in context → produce personal language.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich benutze heute bekannte Satzmuster. | Today I use familiar sentence patterns. |
| Ich spreche in kurzen, klaren Sätzen. | I speak in short, clear sentences. |
| Ich frage und antworte. | I ask and answer. |
| Ich korrigiere meine Fehler. | I correct my mistakes. |

### Common mistakes

- Learning theme words without using them in sentences.
- Avoiding speaking because the lesson contains no new grammar.
- Forgetting older grammar while focusing on vocabulary.

## Perfekt


### Concept

The spoken perfect uses haben or sein plus a past participle at the end. At A1, learn only frequent forms and communication patterns.

### Purpose

Say briefly what happened or what you did.

### Sentence structure

```text
Subject + auxiliary + … + participle. Ich habe gearbeitet. Ich bin gefahren.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich habe gestern gearbeitet. | I worked yesterday. |
| Wir haben einen Film gesehen. | We watched a film. |
| Sie ist nach Hause gegangen. | She went home. |
| Was ist passiert? | What happened? |

### Common mistakes

- Trying to narrate long stories before controlling the present tense.
- Using sein with every movement-related sentence without learning the verb form.
- Forgetting the participle at the end.

### English/Bengali comparison

English past forms are often a single word; German spoken past commonly splits the auxiliary and participle.

### Quick grammar check

1. Copy one example and change one detail.
2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.

## Repair


### Concept

Communication-repair phrases help you continue even when you miss a word or do not understand.

### Purpose

Ask for repetition, slower speech, spelling or clarification.

### Sentence structure

```text
Polite request + specific repair action.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Können Sie das bitte wiederholen? | Could you repeat that, please? |
| Bitte sprechen Sie langsamer. | Please speak more slowly. |
| Wie schreibt man das? | How do you spell that? |
| Was bedeutet das? | What does that mean? |

### Common mistakes

- Pretending to understand and losing the whole conversation.
- Using only Was? in formal situations.
- Switching immediately to English without trying a repair phrase.

### English/Bengali comparison

Polite repair phrases are practical survival tools, not signs of failure.

### Quick grammar check

2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.

## Review


### Concept

Review uses retrieval, correction and performance rather than rereading.

### Purpose

Strengthen memory, locate weak areas and demonstrate the weekly milestone.

### Sentence structure

```text
Recall → test → correct → repeat → perform.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich wiederhole ohne Notizen. | I review without notes. |
| Ich prüfe meine Antworten. | I check my answers. |
| Ich spreche eine Minute. | I speak for one minute. |
| Ich plane meine nächste Wiederholung. | I plan my next review. |

### Common mistakes

- Reading notes without testing yourself.
- Repeating the whole week instead of repairing specific gaps.
- Hiding mistakes instead of recording them.

### English/Bengali comparison

A review day is active training, not a day off and not a punishment.

### Quick grammar check

1. Copy one example and change one detail.
2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.
