## Accusative


### Concept

The accusative marks the direct object. Only masculine singular articles visibly change in the basic article set.

### Purpose

Say what you buy, eat, need, see or want.

### Sentence structure

```text
der → den; ein → einen. die/das/eine/ein normally remain unchanged in the basic accusative.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich kaufe einen Apfel. | I buy an apple. |
| Sie nimmt den Bus. | She takes the bus. |
| Wir brauchen eine Fahrkarte. | We need a ticket. |
| Er bestellt das Menü. | He orders the menu. |

### Common mistakes

- Changing every article in the accusative.
- Forgetting den/einen with masculine direct objects.
- Trying to identify case only by word order.

### English/Bengali comparison

Bengali often marks objects with suffixes or context. German frequently shows the role through the article.

## Negation


### Concept

kein usually negates an indefinite or article-less noun; nicht negates verbs, adjectives, adverbs or specific information.

### Purpose

Say what you do not have, do not want, cannot do or what is not true.

### Sentence structure

```text
kein + noun; nicht often appears late in a simple clause or directly before the element being contrasted.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich habe kein Auto. | I do not have a car. |
| Das ist keine Suppe. | That is not soup. |
| Ich komme heute nicht. | I am not coming today. |
| Der Kaffee ist nicht heiß. | The coffee is not hot. |

### Common mistakes

- Using nicht ein instead of kein.
- Placing nicht mechanically after the subject.
- Forgetting gender and case endings on kein.

### English/Bengali comparison

English uses “not” and “no/not any”; German makes a similar distinction but expresses it through nicht and the ein-like word kein.

1. Copy one example and change one detail.
2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.

## Moechte


### Concept

möchte is the most useful polite A1 form for saying what you would like.

### Purpose

Order food, request a service and state a polite wish.

### Sentence structure

```text
Subject + möchte + object/complement. With another verb: möchte + … + infinitive at the end.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich möchte einen Tee. | I would like a tea. |
| Wir möchten bezahlen. | We would like to pay. |
| Möchten Sie etwas essen? | Would you like something to eat? |
| Ich möchte ein Einzelzimmer reservieren. | I would like to reserve a single room. |

### Common mistakes

- Using ich will in every polite service situation.
- Adding bin before möchte.
- Forgetting the infinitive at the end when a second verb is present.

### English/Bengali comparison

Like Bengali polite requests, tone and wording matter. möchte is safer and softer than a direct command.

2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.

## Review


### Concept

Review uses retrieval, correction and performance rather than rereading.

### Purpose

Strengthen memory, locate weak areas and demonstrate the weekly milestone.

### Sentence structure

```text
Recall → test → correct → repeat → perform.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich wiederhole ohne Notizen. | I review without notes. |
| Ich prüfe meine Antworten. | I check my answers. |
| Ich spreche eine Minute. | I speak for one minute. |
| Ich plane meine nächste Wiederholung. | I plan my next review. |

### Common mistakes

- Reading notes without testing yourself.
- Repeating the whole week instead of repairing specific gaps.
- Hiding mistakes instead of recording them.

### English/Bengali comparison

A review day is active training, not a day off and not a punishment.

### Quick grammar check

1. Copy one example and change one detail.
2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.
