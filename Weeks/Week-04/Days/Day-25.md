## Step 2 ‚ÄĒ Grammar Bite (10 min)

<div class="grammar-box">

### Crucial Distinctions: lernen vs. studieren

### Do Not Confuse: **lernen** vs. **studieren**!

In English, both mean "to study". In German, they have **strict separate meanings**:

1. **lernen** = to learn / study for a test / acquire any skill <SpeakButton text="lernen" />
‚ÄĘ *Ich lerne Deutsch.* <SpeakButton text="Ich lerne Deutsch." />(I am learning German.)
‚ÄĘ *Er lernt f√ľr die Pr√ľfung.* <SpeakButton text="Er lernt f√ľr die Pr√ľfung." />(He is studying for the exam.)

2. **studieren** = to be enrolled at university in a degree subject <SpeakButton text="studieren" />
‚ÄĘ *Sie studiert Medizin.* <SpeakButton text="Sie studiert Medizin." />(She is a university medicine student.)
‚ÄĘ *Ich studiere Informatik.* <SpeakButton text="Ich studiere Informatik." />(I major in Computer Science.)

> <Icon name="alert-triangle" /> **Common Mistake:** Never say *Ich studiere Deutsch* unless you are doing a Bachelor's/Master's degree in German Philology at a university! <SpeakButton text="alert-triangle" />
