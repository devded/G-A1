# Week 5 Grammar — City, Directions, Travel and Basic Dative

## Where


### Concept

wo asks about location, wohin about destination and woher about origin.

### Purpose

Find places, ask directions and discuss movement.

### Sentence structure

```text
Wo ist …? Wohin fährst du? Woher kommst du?
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Wo ist der Bahnhof? | Where is the station? |
| Wohin fährt der Bus? | Where is the bus going? |
| Woher kommt der Zug? | Where does the train come from? |
| Die Apotheke ist dort. | The pharmacy is there. |

### Common mistakes

- Using wo for every movement question.
- Confusing origin with destination.
- Answering a location question with a movement preposition.

### English/Bengali comparison

English often uses “where” for all three ideas; German distinguishes location, destination and origin explicitly.

2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.

## Dative


### Concept

The dative often marks an indirect object and follows several high-frequency prepositions.

### Purpose

Use transport, give information to someone and describe basic locations.

### Sentence structure

```text
der → dem, das → dem, die → der, plural die → den (+ often -n on noun).
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich fahre mit dem Bus. | I travel by bus. |
| Sie spricht mit der Ärztin. | She speaks with the doctor. |
| Ich gebe dem Mann die Karte. | I give the man the card. |
| Wir wohnen bei den Eltern. | We live with the parents. |

### Common mistakes

- Trying to master every dative use at once.
- Forgetting dem for both masculine and neuter.
- Using mit followed by accusative.

### English/Bengali comparison

Bengali can express similar relationships with postpositions. German uses prepositions plus article changes.

### Quick grammar check

1. Copy one example and change one detail.
2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.

## Prepositions


### Concept

At A1, learn common prepositions as complete chunks with their case and meaning.

### Purpose

Describe location, direction, origin, destination and transport.

### Sentence structure

```text
mit + dative; nach + place/direction; zu + dative; von + dative; in/auf with memorised A1 location patterns.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich fahre nach Berlin. | I travel to Berlin. |
| Wir gehen zum Bahnhof. | We go to the station. |
| Der Brief ist von Anna. | The letter is from Anna. |
| Das Buch liegt auf dem Tisch. | The book is on the table. |

### Common mistakes

- Translating one English preposition into one fixed German preposition.
- Forgetting contractions such as zu dem → zum.
- Ignoring the case required by the phrase.

### English/Bengali comparison

Bengali often uses postpositions after the noun; German prepositions come before the noun phrase.

### Quick grammar check

1. Copy one example and change one detail.
2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.

## Integration


### Concept

Today combines previously learned grammar with a practical communication theme.

### Purpose

Move knowledge from controlled exercises into reading, listening, speaking and writing.

### Sentence structure

```text
Review known pattern → add theme vocabulary → practise in context → produce personal language.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich benutze heute bekannte Satzmuster. | Today I use familiar sentence patterns. |
| Ich spreche in kurzen, klaren Sätzen. | I speak in short, clear sentences. |
| Ich frage und antworte. | I ask and answer. |
| Ich korrigiere meine Fehler. | I correct my mistakes. |

### Common mistakes

- Learning theme words without using them in sentences.
- Avoiding speaking because the lesson contains no new grammar.
- Forgetting older grammar while focusing on vocabulary.

### English/Bengali comparison

Integration is where separate pieces become a communication skill.

## Review


### Concept

Review uses retrieval, correction and performance rather than rereading.

### Purpose

Strengthen memory, locate weak areas and demonstrate the weekly milestone.

### Sentence structure

```text
Recall → test → correct → repeat → perform.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich wiederhole ohne Notizen. | I review without notes. |
| Ich prüfe meine Antworten. | I check my answers. |
| Ich spreche eine Minute. | I speak for one minute. |
| Ich plane meine nächste Wiederholung. | I plan my next review. |

### Common mistakes

- Reading notes without testing yourself.
- Repeating the whole week instead of repairing specific gaps.
- Hiding mistakes instead of recording them.

### English/Bengali comparison

A review day is active training, not a day off and not a punishment.

### Quick grammar check

1. Copy one example and change one detail.
2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.
