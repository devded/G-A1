## Adjectives


### Concept

Predicate adjectives after sein remain unchanged. Basic noun-before adjective forms are learned mainly as useful chunks at A1.

### Purpose

Describe people, weather, objects and places.

### Sentence structure

```text
Das Haus ist groß. A common chunk: ein großes Haus.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Das Wetter ist schön. | The weather is nice. |
| Der Mann ist freundlich. | The man is friendly. |
| Sie hat blaue Augen. | She has blue eyes. |
| Das ist ein kleines Zimmer. | That is a small room. |

### Common mistakes

- Adding an ending to a predicate adjective after sein.
- Trying to memorise the complete adjective-declension system at A1.
- Using an English word unchanged as a German adjective.

### English/Bengali comparison

German adjective endings before nouns carry grammatical information not normally marked the same way in Bengali or English.

### Quick grammar check

## Modal Verbs


### Concept

A modal verb is conjugated in position two; the main verb remains an infinitive at the end.

### Purpose

Express ability, necessity, intention and permission.

### Sentence structure

```text
Subject + modal verb + … + infinitive. Ich kann heute kommen.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich kann Deutsch sprechen. | I can speak German. |
| Du musst früh aufstehen. | You must get up early. |
| Wir wollen ins Kino gehen. | We want to go to the cinema. |
| Darf ich hier sitzen? | May I sit here? |

### Common mistakes

- Conjugating both verbs.
- Putting the infinitive immediately after the modal.
- Using zu before the infinitive in these basic modal structures.

### English/Bengali comparison

English also combines modals with a base verb, but German sends the second verb to the end.

### Quick grammar check

1. Copy one example and change one detail.
2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.

## Conjunctions


### Concept

The coordinating conjunctions und, aber, oder and denn connect equal sentence parts without sending the finite verb to the end.

### Purpose

Join ideas and give simple reasons or contrasts.

### Sentence structure

```text
Clause + und/aber/oder/denn + clause with normal main-clause order.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich trinke Tee und er trinkt Kaffee. | I drink tea and he drinks coffee. |
| Es ist kalt, aber sonnig. | It is cold but sunny. |
| Kommst du heute oder morgen? | Are you coming today or tomorrow? |
| Ich bleibe zu Hause, denn ich bin krank. | I stay home because I am ill. |

### Common mistakes

- Moving the verb to the end after these four conjunctions.
- Using denn as if it were a question word in every context.
- Writing extremely long sentences instead of two clear A1 clauses.

### English/Bengali comparison

These work similarly to common English/Bengali coordinating connectors, but punctuation and word order still need attention.

### Quick grammar check

1. Copy one example and change one detail.
2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.

## Comparisons


### Concept

Many adjectives form the comparative with -er; common irregular forms include gut → besser and gern → lieber.

### Purpose

Compare sizes, prices, weather and preferences.

### Sentence structure

```text
A ist größer als B. Ich trinke lieber Tee.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Berlin ist größer als Bonn. | Berlin is bigger than Bonn. |
| Der Bus ist billiger als das Taxi. | The bus is cheaper than the taxi. |
| Heute ist das Wetter besser. | The weather is better today. |
| Ich spiele lieber Fußball. | I prefer playing football. |

### Common mistakes

- Using mehr before every adjective.
- Forgetting als after a comparison.
- Confusing lieber with besser.

### English/Bengali comparison

English often uses -er or more; German also has both patterns but A1 focuses on common forms.

### Quick grammar check

1. Copy one example and change one detail.
2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.

## Review


### Concept

Review uses retrieval, correction and performance rather than rereading.

### Purpose

Strengthen memory, locate weak areas and demonstrate the weekly milestone.

### Sentence structure

```text
Recall → test → correct → repeat → perform.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich wiederhole ohne Notizen. | I review without notes. |
| Ich prüfe meine Antworten. | I check my answers. |
| Ich spreche eine Minute. | I speak for one minute. |
| Ich plane meine nächste Wiederholung. | I plan my next review. |

### Common mistakes

- Reading notes without testing yourself.
- Repeating the whole week instead of repairing specific gaps.
- Hiding mistakes instead of recording them.

### English/Bengali comparison

A review day is active training, not a day off and not a punishment.
