### Modal Verb: k√∂nnen (can) & Modal Sentence Structure

### Conjugation: **k√∂nnen** (can / to be able to)

Notice the vowel change **√∂ ‚Üí a** in singular forms!

| Pronoun | Form | Pronunciation |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| ich <SpeakButton text="ich" /> | k**a**nn <SpeakButton text="kann" /> | /kan/ |
| du <SpeakButton text="du" /> | k**a**nnst <SpeakButton text="kannst" /> | /kanst/ |
| er/sie/es <SpeakButton text="er/sie/es" /> | k**a**nn <SpeakButton text="kann" /> | /kan/ |
| wir <SpeakButton text="wir" /> | k**√∂**nnen <SpeakButton text="k√∂nnen" /> | /Ààk≈ìn…ôn/ |
| ihr <SpeakButton text="ihr" /> | k**√∂**nnt <SpeakButton text="k√∂nnt" /> | /k≈ìnt/ |
| Sie/sie <SpeakButton text="Sie/sie" /> | k**√∂**nnen <SpeakButton text="k√∂nnen" /> | /Ààk≈ìn…ôn/ |

### Modal Sentence Bracket (Satzklammer)

The modal verb goes in **Position 2**, and the main verb goes to the **VERY END in infinitive**!

‚Ä¢ *Wir* **k√∂nnen** *uns am Samstag im Caf√©* **treffen**. <SpeakButton text="Wir k√∂nnen uns am Samstag im Caf√© treffen." />
