# Week 3 Grammar — Time, Routine, Hobbies and Verb Position

## Time Dates


### Concept

German uses um for clock time, am for days and dates, and im for months and seasons.

### Purpose

Arrange appointments and understand schedules.

### Sentence structure

```text
um 8 Uhr; am Montag; am dritten Mai; im Juli; im Winter.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Der Kurs beginnt um neun Uhr. | The course starts at nine. |
| Am Montag arbeite ich. | I work on Monday. |
| Im August habe ich Urlaub. | I have vacation in August. |
| Heute ist der fünfte Juni. | Today is the fifth of June. |

### Common mistakes

- Using one preposition for all time expressions.
- Forgetting ordinal endings in dates.
- Confusing halb acht with 8:30; it means 7:30.

### English/Bengali comparison

German halb acht looks forward to the next hour, which can be surprising to English and Bengali speakers.

3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.

## Irregular Verbs


### Concept

Some common verbs change the stem vowel in du and er/sie/es forms.

### Purpose

Use high-frequency verbs such as fahren, lesen, sehen, sprechen and essen.

### Sentence structure

```text
fahren → du fährst, er fährt; lesen → du liest, er liest. Other persons often keep the regular stem.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Du fährst mit dem Bus. | You travel by bus. |
| Er liest die Zeitung. | He reads the newspaper. |
| Sie spricht Deutsch. | She speaks German. |
| Was isst du? | What are you eating? |

### Common mistakes

- Changing the stem in every person.
- Forgetting the person ending after changing the vowel.
- Treating every verb as irregular.

### English/Bengali comparison

Learn the infinitive together with the du and er/sie/es forms, similar to learning English go/went but within present tense.

## Separable Verbs


### Concept

In a main clause, the prefix of a separable verb moves to the end.

### Purpose

Describe everyday routines such as getting up, shopping, calling and arriving.

### Sentence structure

```text
Subject + conjugated verb stem + … + prefix. aufstehen → Ich stehe um sieben Uhr auf.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich stehe früh auf. | I get up early. |
| Wir kaufen am Samstag ein. | We shop on Saturday. |
| Rufst du mich an? | Are you calling me? |
| Der Zug kommt um zehn Uhr an. | The train arrives at ten. |

### Common mistakes

- Keeping the whole infinitive together in a main clause.
- Putting the prefix immediately after the verb.
- Forgetting that a modal verb keeps the infinitive together at the end.

### English/Bengali comparison

English phrasal verbs provide a loose comparison, but German prefix placement follows a stricter sentence rule.

## Word Order


### Concept

The conjugated verb stays in position two in a normal main clause, even when time or place comes first.

### Purpose

Vary sentences naturally and emphasise when or where something happens.

### Sentence structure

```text
Position 1 + conjugated verb + subject + rest. Heute lerne ich Deutsch.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich arbeite heute. | I work today. |
| Heute arbeite ich. | Today I work. |
| Am Abend sehe ich fern. | In the evening I watch television. |
| In Berlin wohnt meine Schwester. | My sister lives in Berlin. |

### Common mistakes

- Writing Heute ich arbeite.
- Counting each individual word instead of sentence elements.
- Moving the verb to the end because Bengali is often SOV.

### English/Bengali comparison

Bengali commonly places the verb near the end. German main clauses normally require the finite verb in the second slot.

## Review


### Concept

Review uses retrieval, correction and performance rather than rereading.

### Purpose

Strengthen memory, locate weak areas and demonstrate the weekly milestone.

### Sentence structure

```text
Recall → test → correct → repeat → perform.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich wiederhole ohne Notizen. | I review without notes. |
| Ich prüfe meine Antworten. | I check my answers. |
| Ich spreche eine Minute. | I speak for one minute. |
| Ich plane meine nächste Wiederholung. | I plan my next review. |

### Common mistakes

- Reading notes without testing yourself.
- Repeating the whole week instead of repairing specific gaps.
- Hiding mistakes instead of recording them.

### English/Bengali comparison

A review day is active training, not a day off and not a punishment.

2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
