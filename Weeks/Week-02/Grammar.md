# Week 2 Grammar — Family, Possessions, Home and Work

## Haben


### Concept

haben means “to have” and is irregular in du and er/sie/es forms.

### Purpose

Talk about family, possessions, appointments, hunger, thirst and many everyday states.

### Sentence structure

```text
ich habe, du hast, er/sie/es hat, wir haben, ihr habt, Sie/sie haben.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich habe eine Schwester. | I have a sister. |
| Hast du Kinder? | Do you have children? |
| Er hat einen Termin. | He has an appointment. |
| Wir haben Hunger. | We are hungry. |

### Common mistakes

- Using du habe instead of du hast.
- Translating English “I am hungry” word for word; German commonly says Ich habe Hunger.
- Forgetting the article before a countable noun.

### English/Bengali comparison

Like Bengali, German sometimes expresses states through possession: Ich habe Hunger literally means I have hunger.

### Quick grammar check

1. Copy one example and change one detail.
2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.

## Regular Verbs


### Concept

Most present-tense verbs use a stem plus a person ending.

### Purpose

Describe work, study, residence, learning and regular actions.

### Sentence structure

```text
Remove -en: lernen → lern-. Add -e, -st, -t, -en, -t, -en.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich lerne Deutsch. | I learn German. |
| Du arbeitest heute. | You work today. |
| Sie wohnt in Ankara. | She lives in Ankara. |
| Wir machen die Aufgabe. | We do the task. |

### Common mistakes

- Leaving the infinitive unchanged after ich or du.
- Dropping an extra e in verbs such as arbeiten: du arbeitest.
- Using English continuous forms unnecessarily.

### English/Bengali comparison

Bengali verbs also change according to person and politeness, but the German ending pattern is different and should be memorised as a table.

### Quick grammar check

1. Copy one example and change one detail.
2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.

## Articles Gender


### Concept

German nouns have grammatical gender: der, die or das. Gender is part of the vocabulary item.

### Purpose

Use nouns accurately and prepare for case changes later.

### Sentence structure

```text
Learn article + noun + plural together: der Tisch – die Tische.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| der Vater | the father |
| die Mutter | the mother |
| das Kind | the child |
| die Kinder | the children |

### Common mistakes

- Learning only the noun.
- Assuming natural gender always predicts grammatical gender.
- Using capitalisation inconsistently.

### English/Bengali comparison

Bengali and English do not assign this three-way grammatical gender to everyday nouns, so memorisation with the article is essential.

### Quick grammar check

1. Copy one example and change one detail.
2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.

## Plurals Possessives


### Concept

German plural forms vary. Possessive determiners behave like ein-words and agree with the noun context.

### Purpose

Talk about several people or objects and show ownership or relationships.

### Sentence structure

```text
mein/dein/sein/ihr/Ihr + noun; learn plural individually rather than relying on one rule.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| mein Bruder | my brother |
| meine Schwester | my sister |
| meine Eltern | my parents |
| Ihr Name | your name, formal |

### Common mistakes

- Adding -s to every plural.
- Using mein before every noun regardless of gender.
- Confusing ihr meaning her/their with Ihr meaning your, formal.

### English/Bengali comparison

Bengali possessives do not change for grammatical gender. German mein/meine changes according to the following noun.

### Quick grammar check

1. Copy one example and change one detail.
2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.

## Review


### Concept

Review uses retrieval, correction and performance rather than rereading.

### Purpose

Strengthen memory, locate weak areas and demonstrate the weekly milestone.

### Sentence structure

```text
Recall → test → correct → repeat → perform.
```

### Core examples

| German | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ich wiederhole ohne Notizen. | I review without notes. |
| Ich prüfe meine Antworten. | I check my answers. |
| Ich spreche eine Minute. | I speak for one minute. |
| Ich plane meine nächste Wiederholung. | I plan my next review. |

### Common mistakes

- Reading notes without testing yourself.
- Repeating the whole week instead of repairing specific gaps.
- Hiding mistakes instead of recording them.

### English/Bengali comparison

A review day is active training, not a day off and not a punishment.

### Quick grammar check

1. Copy one example and change one detail.
2. Say the new sentence aloud twice.
3. Write one sentence about your real life.
4. Cover the rule and explain it in your own words.
