# Nouns, Articles and Gender

## Learning purpose

Use articles as part of each noun and recognise the subject form.


### Concept

German nouns have grammatical gender: der, die or das. Gender is part of the vocabulary item.

### Purpose

Use nouns accurately and prepare for case changes later.

### Sentence structure

```text
Learn article + noun + plural together: der Tisch – die Tische.
```

### Common mistakes

- Learning only the noun.
- Assuming natural gender always predicts grammatical gender.
- Using capitalisation inconsistently.

### English/Bengali comparison

Bengali and English do not assign this three-way grammatical gender to everyday nouns, so memorisation with the article is essential.

## Bengali comparison

Bengali nouns do not normally carry grammatical-gender articles. German requires the article to be learned as part of the word: `der Tisch`, `die Lampe`, `das Bett`.

## Useful gender clues

Clues help but are not perfect rules. Many nouns ending in `-ung`, `-heit`, `-keit` and `-schaft` are feminine. Diminutives ending in `-chen` are neuter. Learn every noun with its article rather than guessing permanently.
